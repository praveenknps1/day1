Bruce Lee

Bruce Lee was a martial artist, actor, and cultural icon renowned for his martial arts skills, charisma, and philosophy. He revolutionized the portrayal of Asians in Hollywood and popularized martial arts worldwide through his films, most notably "Enter the Dragon." Born in San Francisco and raised in Hong Kong, Lee developed his own martial art philosophy called Jeet Kune Do, emphasizing efficiency, directness, and personal expression. His influence extended beyond martial arts into areas like fitness, philosophy, and pop culture. Despite his untimely death at age 32, Bruce Lee's legacy remains influential, inspiring generations of martial artists and fans around the globe.

Sundar Pichai

Sundar Pichai is the CEO of Alphabet Inc., the parent company of Google, as well as the CEO of Google LLC. He joined Google in 2004 and has been instrumental in the development of several key products, including Chrome, Gmail, and Google Drive. Pichai became CEO of Google in 2015, succeeding Larry Page, and later assumed the role of Alphabet's CEO in 2019. Pichai is recognized for his strategic vision and leadership in steering Google's expansion into various sectors, including artificial intelligence, cloud computing, and hardware. He's widely regarded as one of the most influential figures in the tech industry today.

Major Sandeep Unnikrishnan

Major Sandeep Unnikrishnan was an Indian Army officer, serving in the elite Special Action Group of the National Security Guard (NSG). He tragically lost his life during the 2008 Mumbai attacks while leading his team in the rescue operation at the Taj Mahal Palace Hotel. His bravery and selflessness in the face of danger earned him widespread admiration and posthumously the Ashoka Chakra, India's highest peacetime gallantry award. Major Sandeep Unnikrishnan's legacy continues to inspire generations of Indians, symbolizing the courage and sacrifice of the armed forces in protecting the nation.
